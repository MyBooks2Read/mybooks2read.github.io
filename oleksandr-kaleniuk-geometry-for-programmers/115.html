91 4.1 Some special cases of geometric transformations But first, I should probably give you a small reminder of what sine and cosine func- tions are. Figure 4.6 shows a sine. A sine is a continuous function, with the domain being and the codomain being the interval [–1, 1]. It’s periodic, which means that it copies itself every 2π as it goes along the x-axis. The sine is odd, meaning that its left half, where all the xs are negative, is exactly the opposite of the right half, where all the xs are positive: sin(–x) = –sin(x) Figure 4.5 Three examples of standard square rotations.

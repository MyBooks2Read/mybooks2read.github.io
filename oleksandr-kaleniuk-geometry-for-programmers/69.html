We’ll use matrices to build functions with desirable properties in chapter 6, and in chapter 12, we’ll discuss how the field of computer tomography is essentially solving huge matrix equations. 2.5.3 Section 2.5 summary Matrices are like 2D vectors: tables of numbers with specific operations that define matrix algebra. A matrix operation may have some geometrical meaning. A matrix-on- vector multiplication, for example, is a projective transformation. Tensor is the term that generalizes vectors and matrices. Tensors are like multidi- mensional arrays, but with their own algebras (sets of rules and operations).

363 11.3 Practical example: Contouring with marching cubes and dual contouring algorithms Now let’s get back to the question of what dual means. As you may have noticed, with marching cubes, we analyzed cells to find edges that represent the modeled curve. With dual contouring, we analyze edges to find the cells. With marching cubes, we optimized vertices positions on the found edges. With dual contouring, we optimized the vertices’ positions in the found cells. Conceptually, this algorithm is the same, but it’s as though someone swapped cells and edges around in the description. That’s what makes dual contouring dual.

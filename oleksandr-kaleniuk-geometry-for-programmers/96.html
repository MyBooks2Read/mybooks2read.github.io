The principle of changing a matrix equation until it becomes a solution scales nicely to higher dimensions. No matter how many dimensions you have, you can always con- vert your coefficients matrix to an identity matrix by using only three operations:  You can scale a row and a free coefficient by some nonzero number.  You can add one row and a free coefficient to another row and a free coefficient.  You can swap two rows and the respective free coefficients.

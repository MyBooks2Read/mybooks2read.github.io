374 Modeling bodies with images and voxels A 3D image is an image, pretty much like any bitmap but with another dimension. As you can imagine a 2D image as an array of strings in which every string is an array of colored elements, you can imagine a 3D image as an array of 2D images (figure 12.1). 2D images are made of colored squares called pixels, and 3D images are made out of colored cubes called voxels. The term voxel means a pixel that has a volume: volume pixel, vo-xel, voxel. The term image stack is often used interchangeably with 3D image. The term has a slightly broader meaning, though.

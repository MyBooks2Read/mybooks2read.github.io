57 3.3 A visual example of an interactive linear solver 3.3.2 Starting point and exit conditions Now we know how to get closer to the point of the solution, but that knowledge isn’t enough to craft a workable iterative algorithm. We also need to think about where we start iterating from and—most important—how we stop. Is the starting position relevant to our task? Not really. Ideally, of course, we want it to be as close to the solution as possible, but we don’t know where that might be. That’s the whole point.

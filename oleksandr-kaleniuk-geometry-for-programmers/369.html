This operation is possible but doesn’t come as cheap in terms of performance and complexity, as it does with SDFs. You can compose surface generation, Boolean opera- tions, and offsets to make the shape of the body you want. Almost every operation you see in a CAD application’s toolbox is a combination of these three, including chamfer- ing and filleting (figure 11.3). To apply a chamfer to a model, you pick an edge, find the edge’s offset on the cube’s surface, connect the offset lines with a surface, and then remove the pieces of the cube that are on the wrong side of this new surface (figure 11.4). A fillet is almost the same, but the surface between the offset lines is guided by a Bézier function that respects the initial tangents.

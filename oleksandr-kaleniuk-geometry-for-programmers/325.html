Geometrically, it’s also the same as the straightforward solution with a Googleable plane equation. You simply have to look at it from another perspective. Yes, if you divide the volume of the parallelepiped by the area of its base parallelo- gram, you get its height. At the same time, the cross product (p 2 – p 1 ) × (p 3 – p 1 ) pro- duces the normal vector to the plane that hosts both the (p 2 – p 1 ) and (p 3 – p 1 ) vectors. When you divide this normal vector by its length ||(p 2 – p 1 ) × (p 3 – p 1 )||, you get a normalized vector: the vector with length 1. When you get a dot product of this normalized normal vector and the vector from the point p to one of the points on the plane, you get the wanted distance. Knowing the geometric meaning of vector products, you can opt out of third-party dependencies and implement the most efficient solution on the spot. Even if you choose to look for a solution somewhere, the formulas you’ll find out there are often easier to understand when you keep their geometrical sense in mind.

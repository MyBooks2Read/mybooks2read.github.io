This approach scales nicely to any degree of polynomials and also to any number of dimensions. You can make trilinear or triquadratic transformations in 3D. Moreover, with this approach, the transformation doesn’t have to be just bi- or tri-something. It could be linear by one axis and cubic by the other, for example, so it could be linear- cubic.

One important thing: with rational Bézier curves, we can describe conic sections such as circles, ellipses, and hyperbolas. At the same time, rational functions are more com- putationally heavy because of all the extra multiplications and one extra division. That’s why in 2D graphics or typography, regular polynomial Bézier curves are still preferable, whereas rational Bézier curves are indispensable in computer-aided design (CAD) applications, in which you need precision more than speed.

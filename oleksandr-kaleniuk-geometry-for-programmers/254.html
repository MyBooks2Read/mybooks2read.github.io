Also, a simple proportion makes Bézier points into tangent vec- tors for explicit polynomial modeling, and vice versa. The proportion is three. Yes, it’s that simple. The tangent vectors are three times the point differences (figure 7.17). This correspondence is nice, of course, because it allows us to build smooth com- plex curves out of cubic Béziers as we can already do with explicit polynomials.
